Jackson High School's Mission Statement

Our mission is to provide a rigorous curriculum that sets high standards and prepares all students for the future.

School History

Henry M. Jackson High School, which is widely referred to as Jackson High School, opened its doors in 1994 as a public high school in the Everett School District. Named after Henry M. Jackson, the late Washington-native U.S Senator, this school has quickly flourished to serve 2,196 students. Despite this large number, the graduation rate for Jackson High School has come to an all-time high of 95% due to the perseverance of both staff and students.
